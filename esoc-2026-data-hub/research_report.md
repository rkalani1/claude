## 1. Executive Clinical Takeaways

**Practice-changing.** OCEANIC-STROKE (asundexian, NEJM 2026;394:1467–1479) is now the strongest secondary-prevention signal in non-cardioembolic ischaemic stroke since CHANCE/POINT/THALES established short-term DAPT. The 50 mg oral Factor XIa inhibitor cuts recurrent ischaemic stroke (csHR 0.74, 95% CI 0.65–0.84, p<0.001) and — critically — does so without an excess of intracranial haemorrhage (haemorrhagic transformation 0.3% in both arms). The four ESOC 2026 sub-analyses (LB157–LB160) sharpen the clinical picture: not only fewer strokes, but recurrences are less severe (NIHSS ≥8 in 22.9% vs 30.3%) and ~31% less disabling. Practice impact will hinge on FDA/EMA decisions and post-marketing safety, but this is the first FXIa inhibitor with a coherent efficacy + safety story in stroke.

**Practice-changing (consolidating).** The ATLAS Collaboration IPD meta-analysis (LT88) of large-core thrombectomy (n=1,886 across RESCUE-Japan LIMIT, ANGEL-ASPECT, SELECT2, TENSION, TESLA, LASTE) reports an adjusted shift OR 1.63 (95% CI 1.42–1.87, p<0.001) with consistent benefit across age, sex, NIHSS, time-to-treatment, stroke size, and mismatch profile. This consolidates the post-2022 paradigm and resolves residual subgroup uncertainty. Functional independence rates remain low in absolute terms (9.5% vs 7.5% mRS 0–2), but the shift effect is substantial and the consistency across geographies is reassuring.

**Practice-changing (parent), pending substudy.** TRIDENT (NEJM 2026, 22 Apr 2026, DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2515043) demonstrates that low-dose triple-pill (telmisartan 20 + indapamide 1.25 + amlodipine 2.5 mg) cuts recurrent stroke ~40% with mean SBP reduction ~9 mmHg and no excess ICH after spontaneous intracerebral haemorrhage. The ESOC 2026 cognitive substudy (LB128) extends this to dementia/cognition outcomes, but specific cognitive numerical results are not in the indexed press surface yet.

**Near-practice-changing.** CHILL-ART (LT70, n=262, 26 Chinese centres) is the first multicentre RCT to show that adding intra-arterial cold-saline cooling to EVT improves 90-day functional independence (mRS 0–2: 54.7% vs 39.8%; aRR 1.36, 95% CI 1.05–1.76; p=0.018) without increasing sICH (7.0% vs 9.0%) or 90-day mortality (13.3% vs 18.0%). Caveat: companion FOCUS trial (LT77, n=258, 12 Chinese centres) was negative on functional outcome (acOR 1.16, 95% CI 0.75–1.79, p=0.51) but corroborated a strong ICH-reduction safety signal (aRD −0.174, 95% CI −0.288 to −0.059, p=0.003). The split primary-efficacy results across two near-simultaneous Chinese trials means IA cooling is not yet ready for guideline change without IPD reconciliation and Western validation.

**Near-practice-changing (mixed).** CASES (LB22, n=597, 26 Dutch/Belgian centres, BeNeFIT consortium) tested immediate vs deferred carotid stenting during EVT for tandem lesions. Immediate CAS was non-inferior in the per-protocol set (acOR 1.10, 95% CI 0.81–1.50) but FAILED non-inferiority in the FAS/ITT (acOR 1.06, 95% CI 0.79–1.43; lower bound 0.79 below the pre-specified NI margin of 0.80). Safety was favourable for immediate CAS (sICH 2.1% vs 3.8%; 90-day mortality 21% vs 25%). The PP-vs-FAS divergence is the central methodological finding: in NI trials, the FAS is the conservative answer and its failure is clinically authoritative. Practice impact: safety reassurance for immediate CAS, but non-inferiority is not established in the conservative analysis.

**Near-practice-changing (device endpoint).** DISTALS (LB146, n=149) demonstrated dramatic 24-h reperfusion advantage for the Tigertriever 13 stentriever in DMVO (86.3% vs 27.7% control, p<0.001) with 0% sICH. However, the broader DMVO thrombectomy literature (DISTAL, ESCAPE-MeVO) has shown NO functional benefit despite excellent reperfusion. DISTALS reports a reperfusion endpoint, not a 90-day functional outcome — this device should be viewed as enabling distal thrombectomy, not justifying it.

**Neutral / negative.** MASTERSTROKE (LB116, n=562) showed no difference in 90-day mRS shift (median mRS 2 in both arms, p=0.49) with intra-procedural SBP target 140 vs 170 mmHg during EVT under GA. No difference in sICH or mortality. Class I evidence supporting current AHA/ESO 140–180 mmHg recommendation. TECNO (LT42) showed 3 mg IA tenecteplase added after incomplete EVT did NOT improve early reperfusion (39% vs 36%, adjusted RD +3.4%, 95% CI −15% to 21.7%, NS) or late reperfusion (51% vs 40%, NS), with sICH 5.2% vs 8.6%. Most notable: 90-day mRS 0–2 was numerically inferior in the TNK arm (27% vs 38%, acOR 0.60, 95% CI 0.34–1.06) — a hypothesis-worsening signal. CRAFT (LT22, n=1,676) showed intensive home SBP <120 mmHg in AF did NOT reduce major CV events vs <135 mmHg (win ratio 1.02, 95% CI 0.90–1.15, p=0.76); concerning numerical pattern with MORE strokes (70 vs 54) and MORE MIs (16 vs 4) in the intensive arm raises J-curve concerns.

**Hypothesis-generating / source-limited (Day-3 trials).** As of the extraction date, the Day 3 (Friday 8 May) ESO press release was not yet posted. Trials confirmed presented in the Day-3 LCT session per the official Curtain Raiser but without publicly available numerical primary-endpoint results include: TNK-MeVO, ATTRACTION, ATILA, GREEN (glenzocimab), GALLOP-2 (GLP-1 agonist), and EXCOA-CVT. EXTEND-IA DNase, LATE-MT, and MILD-MT are similarly source-limited. Logged in the studies array and skipped/insufficient-source ledger pending data release.

**Population-level signals.** Pantoja-Ruiz / South London Stroke Register (30-year cohort, 7,726 strokes among ~333,000 population): stroke incidence rose 13% in 2020–2024 after a 34% prior decline, with stark ethnic disparities — IRR 2.31 Black African vs White, IRR 2.00 Black Caribbean vs White. Penn / UK Biobank (n=460,000, 14-year follow-up, 12,290 strokes): U-shaped resting heart rate-stroke risk relationship, nadir 60–69 bpm, +25% risk for HR <50 bpm, +45% risk for HR ≥90 bpm.

## 2. Methods and Source Strategy

The extraction traversed five nested concentric source layers in this order, with deduplication at each level.

**Layer 1 — Official conference surface.** ESOC 2026 main page (eso-stroke.org/esoc2026/), Scientific Programme, Press Portal, Abstract Books, Conference Highlights, Related Journals and Publications, and the official ESOC 2026 Curtain Raiser PDF. The Curtain Raiser is the authoritative pre-conference trial-by-day preview; it was used to seed the master candidate list of named trials (EXTEND-IA DNase, MASTERSTROKE, TECNO, CHILL-ART, FOCUS, AVERT-DOSE, OCEANIC-STROKE additional analyses; ATLAS, LATE-MT, MILD-MT, DISTALS, CASES, CRAFT, COMMITS; TRIDENT, TNK-MeVO, ATTRACTION, ATILA, GREEN, GALLOP-2, EXCOA-CVT). Stale ESOC 2024 (Basel) "Curtain Raiser" PDFs (Curtain-Raiser-1 through 4) and the leftover 2024 trial preview list embedded in the Programme Tracks page were identified and explicitly excluded.

**Layer 2 — Official press releases.** Day 1 (6 May) and Day 2 (7 May) ESO press releases were fetched as PDFs and parsed for verbatim numerical primary-endpoint results. These cover, in full quantitative detail, MASTERSTROKE, TECNO, AVERT-DOSE, OCEANIC-STROKE update, CHILL-ART, FOCUS, CASES, CRAFT, and COMMITS. The Day 3 press release was not yet posted at extraction time. Two additional ESO press briefings (Pantoja-Ruiz / SLSR; Penn / UK Biobank) were fetched and parsed.

**Layer 3 — Master abstract supplement.** European Stroke Journal Volume 11 Supplement_1, May 2026, hosted at academic.oup.com/esj/issue/11/Supplement_1, comprises 14 paginated TOC pages covering Plenary Orals (~155 entries), YSPR (4 entries), Posters and ePosters (~1,500+), and Late-Breaking sections that include LB-prefix (~218 entries), LT-prefix Late-Breaking Trials (~91 entries), and OT-prefix Ongoing/Trial-in-Progress (~202 entries). Pages 1–5, 7–9, 11–14 were fetched and parsed; pages 6 and 10 were not directly fetched (they fall in the Posters/ePosters section and the LB/LT/OT inventory was complete at pages 12–14). The abstract numbering scheme (ESOC2026A####, ESOC2026YS###, ESOC2026LB###, ESOC2026LT###, ESOC2026OT###) was decoded and used to stratify candidates.

**Layer 4 — Concurrent publications and registry data.** PubMed and Crossref searches for the named trials returned the OCEANIC-STROKE primary publication (Sharma M et al., NEJM 2026;394:1467–1479, DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2513880) and TRIDENT parent trial (NEJM 2026, DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2515043). The TECNO methodology paper (ESJ, DOI 10.1177/23969873251381974) and CHILL-ART protocol (Stroke: Vascular and Interventional Neurology, DOI 10.1161/SVIN.125.002058) were retrieved. ClinicalTrials.gov registry pages were retrieved for every named trial with a known NCT identifier.

**Layer 5 — Secondary press analyses.** TCTMD (Todd Neale, 30 Apr 2026 preview, plus the conference page), Neuro News International (TECNO, CASES, ATLAS), Endovascular Today (DISTALS), Medscape (ATLAS), BusinessWire (Bayer ESOC 2026, Rapid Medical DISTALS), and George Medicines press release (TRIDENT) were used to triangulate numerical results, never as the sole numerical source for an included study.

**Inclusion threshold.** Studies were included in the appraised studies array if the ESOC 2026 numerical primary-endpoint result was publicly available via at least one Layer 1–4 source. Studies confirmed presented but lacking quantitative data in the public surface as of the extraction date were tagged practice_impact_category = "source-limited" and retained in the studies array (rather than being moved to the skipped ledger) when the registry / Curtain Raiser provided enough bibliographic detail to identify them.

**Exclusion.** Plenary, YSPR, Poster, and ePoster abstracts whose full numerical content lives only in individual ESJ article pages and not in the press surface; trial-in-progress (OT-prefix) protocols; non-clinical-trial educational/QI/audit/qualitative items; and stale ESOC 2024 carry-over content were logged in the skipped ledger.

## 4. Practice-Changing or Near-Practice-Changing Trials

### OCEANIC-STROKE additional analyses (LB157–LB160) — practice-changing
- **Question:** Does asundexian 50 mg PO daily reduce recurrent ischaemic stroke after non-cardioembolic stroke or high-risk TIA without excess bleeding?
- **Design:** International phase III, double-blind, placebo-controlled, event-driven RCT in 702 sites/37 countries. 12,327 randomised. Median follow-up 567 days.
- **Population:** Acute non-cardioembolic ischaemic stroke or high-risk TIA within 72 h, on standard antiplatelet therapy.
- **Intervention/control:** Asundexian 50 mg vs placebo, both on top of antiplatelet.
- **Key result:** csHR 0.74 (95% CI 0.65–0.84, p<0.001) for first recurrent ischaemic stroke; 6.2% vs 8.4%; ARR 1.9% at 1 year. Severe stroke (NIHSS ≥8 among recurrers) 22.9% vs 30.3%; disabling stroke ~31% RRR.
- **Safety:** Haemorrhagic transformation 0.3% in both arms. ISTH major bleeding 1.9% vs 1.7% (parent trial). No excess ICH.
- **Implication:** First FXIa inhibitor with coherent efficacy + safety in stroke. Practice-changing pending regulatory and guideline integration.
- **Confidence:** High. NEJM publication available; ESOC 2026 sub-analyses verbatim from official ESO press release.

### ATLAS Collaboration (LT88) — practice-changing (consolidating)
- **Question:** Does mechanical thrombectomy benefit patients with anterior LVO and large infarct core?
- **Design:** IPD meta-analysis of 6 randomised trials (RESCUE-Japan LIMIT, ANGEL-ASPECT, SELECT2, TENSION, TESLA, LASTE). n=1,886.
- **Key result:** Adjusted OR for 90-d mRS shift 1.63 (95% CI 1.42–1.87, p<0.001). aRR 2.61 for mRS 0–2; absolute mRS 0–2 9.5% vs 7.5%. Benefit consistent across age, sex, NIHSS, time-to-treatment, stroke size, mismatch profile.
- **Safety:** Higher sICH with EVT, lower mortality with EVT. Net benefit positive.
- **Implication:** Resolves residual subgroup uncertainty. Supports broad EVT application across the large-core spectrum.
- **Confidence:** Moderate. Manuscript-specific DOI for the ESOC 2026 readout not yet indexed.

### TRIDENT parent trial + cognitive substudy (LB128) — practice-changing (parent)
- **Question:** Does low-dose triple antihypertensive pill prevent recurrent stroke and cognitive decline after spontaneous ICH?
- **Design:** International double-blind RCT, n≈1,670.
- **Key result (parent NEJM 2026):** ~40% RRR for recurrent stroke, mean SBP reduction ~9 mmHg, ~33% MACE reduction, no excess ICH.
- **Cognitive substudy at ESOC 2026:** Specific numerical cognitive endpoint results not in indexed press release.
- **Confidence:** Moderate. Parent NEJM publication (DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2515043) is high-confidence; cognitive substudy pending.

### CHILL-ART (LT70) — near-practice-changing
- **Question:** Does intra-arterial cold-saline cooling during EVT improve 90-day functional independence?
- **Design:** Multicentre RCT, 26 Chinese centres, n=262. Cold-saline + EVT vs room-temperature saline (sham) + EVT.
- **Population:** Anterior LVO, age 18–85, treated <24 h.
- **Key result:** mRS 0–2 at 90 d 54.7% vs 39.8%; adjusted RR 1.36 (95% CI 1.05–1.76; p=0.018). Effect direction robust but lower CI bound 1.05 fragile.
- **Safety:** sICH 7.0% vs 9.0% (NS); 90-d mortality 13.3% vs 18.0% (NS).
- **Caveat:** Companion FOCUS trial negative on primary efficacy.
- **Implication:** If reproducible in Western populations and the FOCUS discrepancy is reconciled, IA cooling could be a practice-changing adjunct. Currently near-practice-changing.

### CASES (LB22) — near-practice-changing
- **Question:** Is immediate carotid artery stenting non-inferior to deferred treatment for tandem-lesion EVT?
- **Design:** Open-label multicentre RCT, BeNeFIT consortium, 26 NL/BE centres, n=597.
- **Key result:** FAS acOR 1.06 (95% CI 0.79–1.43) — NI not met (lower bound below pre-specified margin 0.80). PP acOR 1.10 (95% CI 0.81–1.50) — non-inferior in PP only.
- **Safety:** sICH 2.1% vs 3.8% (OR 0.55, 95% CI 0.29–1.40); 90-d mortality 21% vs 25% (acOR 0.79, 95% CI 0.54–1.14). Both NS but directionally favourable.
- **Implication:** Safety reassurance for immediate CAS, but FAS-failed NI means individualised decision-making remains standard.

### DISTALS (LB146) — near-practice-changing (device endpoint)
- **Question:** Does the low-profile Tigertriever 13 stent retriever achieve reperfusion in DMVO patients ineligible for IV thrombolysis?
- **Design:** Multicentre RCT, n=149. TGT13 thrombectomy vs medical management alone.
- **Key result:** 24-h reperfusion 86.3% vs 27.7% (p<0.001); 0% sICH in TGT13 arm.
- **Caveat:** Reperfusion endpoint, not 90-d functional outcome. Broader DMVO literature (DISTAL, ESCAPE-MeVO) shows no functional benefit despite excellent reperfusion.
- **Implication:** Device approval pathway likely; clinical adoption requires 90-d functional data.

## 5. Neutral, Negative, or Hypothesis-Generating Trials

### MASTERSTROKE (LB116) — neutral / negative (and reassuring)
- **Question:** Does intra-procedural SBP target 140 vs 170 mmHg during EVT under GA affect mRS shift at 90 d?
- **Result:** Median mRS 2 in both arms, p=0.49. No difference in sICH or 90-d mortality.
- **n:** 562, median NIHSS 15.
- **Implication:** Class I evidence supporting current AHA/ESO 140–180 mmHg recommendation. Anaesthetists do not need to chase a tighter target during the case.

### TECNO (LT42) — neutral / negative
- **Question:** Does 3 mg IA tenecteplase improve reperfusion after incomplete EVT?
- **Result:** Early reperfusion 39% vs 36% (adjusted RD +3.4%, 95% CI −15% to 21.7%, NS); late 51% vs 40% (RD +15.4%, 95% CI −3.3% to 32.9%, NS); 90-d mRS 0–2 27% vs 38% (acOR 0.60, 95% CI 0.34–1.06). sICH 5.2% vs 8.6%.
- **Implication:** PI Kaesmacher: routine IA tenecteplase as adjunct for incomplete reperfusion is not supported. The mRS 0–2 acOR 0.60 is a hypothesis-worsening signal beyond the primary-endpoint failure.

### CRAFT (LT22) — neutral / negative
- **Question:** Does intensive home SBP <120 mmHg vs <135 mmHg in AF reduce major CV events?
- **Result:** Win ratio 1.02 (95% CI 0.90–1.15, p=0.76); time-to-first-event HR 0.97 (95% CI 0.76–1.25). Achieved separation 8.3 mmHg.
- **Concerning numerical pattern:** Intensive vs standard had MORE strokes (70 vs 54) and MORE MIs (16 vs 4) but fewer CV deaths (27 vs 33) and fewer HF hospitalisations (104 vs 137). Possible J-curve effect of aggressive BP lowering.
- **n:** 1,676 (838 per arm), 157 hospitals China/Japan, mean 2.5-y follow-up.

### FOCUS (LT77) — neutral on primary, positive on safety
- **Question:** Does selective IA cooling during EVT improve 90-d mRS shift?
- **Result:** acOR 1.16 (95% CI 0.75–1.79, p=0.51) — NS. Any 24-h ICH aRD −0.174 (95% CI −0.288 to −0.059, p=0.003) — favoured cooling.
- **n:** 258, 12 Chinese centres.
- **Implication:** Negative primary, positive safety. Combined with CHILL-ART, suggests cooling may protect the BBB but functional benefit is uncertain.

### COMMITS (LT87) — hypothesis-generating
- **Question:** Does motivational interviewing prevent post-stroke depression?
- **Status:** Numerical primary outcome not yet in press release. n=1,246, 16 UK stroke units.
- **Design strength:** 3-arm with attention control isolates specific MIBI effect.

### AVERT-DOSE (LT38) — hypothesis-generating
- **Question:** What is the optimal dose of early mobility training in mild/moderate ischaemic stroke?
- **Status:** Stopped early for funding. Primary outcome data to be presented separately by stroke severity. n=1,000 across 50 hospitals/7 countries.

## 6. High-Impact Observational, Registry, and Secondary Analyses

### Pantoja-Ruiz / South London Stroke Register (press briefing)
- Population-based registry, ~333,000 catchment, 7,726 strokes over 30 years (1995–2024).
- Stroke incidence ROSE 13% in 2020–2024 after a 34% prior decline.
- IRR 2.31 Black African vs White; IRR 2.00 Black Caribbean vs White.
- **Confounding caveats:** Single UK region; 95% CIs not in press summary. Subtype-specific incidence not reported.

### Penn et al. UK Biobank heart-rate cohort (press briefing)
- n=460,000, 14-year follow-up, 12,290 strokes.
- U-shaped HR-stroke risk: nadir 60–69 bpm; +25% risk <50 bpm; +45% risk ≥90 bpm.
- **Confounding caveats:** No adjustment for rate-controlling medications reported. The bradycardia signal could reflect confounding by indication (beta-blockers, AF, heart failure). Single-time HR exposure misclassification risk.

### ATLAS Collaboration (LT88)
- IPD meta-analysis (already covered in Section 4). Contains the most rigorous current subgroup-heterogeneity evidence for large-core thrombectomy.

## 9. Study-by-Study Critical Appraisals

The appraisal template below is applied to every study in the studies array. To preserve report compactness, the long-form appraisal text is rendered in the JSON file (`esoc_data.json`, `critical_appraisal` object) and on the dashboard cards (expandable sections); the report includes condensed summaries.

### OCEANIC-STROKE
- **Bottom line:** First convincing FXIa inhibitor signal in stroke. csHR 0.74 (0.65–0.84). No excess bleeding. Practice-changing pending regulatory action.
- **Internal validity:** High (phase III double-blind, 702 sites, 37 countries).
- **Statistical:** Tight CI excluding 1.0; consistency across 4 ESOC sub-analyses supports robustness.
- **Bias threats:** Low across selection, performance, detection. Crossover minimal in event-driven design.
- **Practice impact:** Practice-changing.
- **What I'd need:** FDA/EMA approval, head-to-head vs short-term DAPT, cost data, reversal algorithm, predictors-analysis effect estimates.

### MASTERSTROKE
- **Bottom line:** No benefit, no harm of intra-procedural SBP 140 vs 170 mmHg during EVT under GA. Class I evidence supporting current 140–180 mmHg recommendation.
- **Statistical:** p=0.49 for shift, median mRS identical. Power adequate.
- **Critical missing:** Achieved SBP separation between arms not in press summary — the most important methodological detail for interpreting a null BP-target trial.
- **Practice impact:** Neutral / confirmatory.

### TECNO
- **Bottom line:** Negative on co-primary reperfusion endpoints (early adjusted RD +3.4% [−15 to 21.7]; late +15.4% [−3.3 to 32.9]); 90-d mRS 0–2 numerically inferior (acOR 0.60, 0.34–1.06). Not just neutral — hypothesis-worsening on functional outcome.
- **Practice impact:** Negative.

### AVERT-DOSE
- **Bottom line:** Largest acute mobility-dose-finding RCT (n=1,000, 50 hospitals, 7 countries). Stopped early for funding. Primary outcome data to be presented separately by stroke severity. Practice impact pending full results.

### CHILL-ART
- **Bottom line:** Positive: mRS 0–2 54.7% vs 39.8% (aRR 1.36, 1.05–1.76, p=0.018), no safety concern. Caveat: companion FOCUS negative.
- **What I'd need:** FOCUS reconciliation, Western validation, full publication with cooling-protocol details, MRI imaging endpoints.

### FOCUS
- **Bottom line:** Negative on primary functional outcome (acOR 1.16, 0.75–1.79), positive on 24-h ICH (aRD −0.174, p=0.003).
- **Practice impact:** Functional benefit not established; safety/BBB-protection signal real but not actionable alone.

### CASES
- **Bottom line:** PP: non-inferior (acOR 1.10, 0.81–1.50). FAS: NI not met (acOR 1.06, 0.79–1.43, lower bound below 0.80 margin). Safety favourable for immediate CAS.
- **Critical methodological note:** In NI trials, FAS is the conservative analysis. Failure in FAS = formal conclusion of "non-inferiority not established."

### CRAFT
- **Bottom line:** Negative (win ratio 1.02, 0.90–1.15, p=0.76). Concerning numerical pattern: more strokes (70 vs 54) and MIs (16 vs 4) in intensive arm.
- **Subgroup:** Highly significant heterogeneity by sex/age — younger males may benefit. But subgroup signal alone should not drive practice change.

### COMMITS
- **Bottom line:** Numerical primary outcome not yet in press release. 3-arm design (MIBI vs attention control vs usual care) is methodologically optimal.

### DISTALS
- **Bottom line:** Stunning reperfusion advantage (86.3% vs 27.7%, p<0.001) with 0% sICH. BUT 24-h reperfusion endpoint, not 90-d functional. DMVO functional-outcome literature (DISTAL, ESCAPE-MeVO) is negative.
- **Practice impact:** Device approval likely; clinical adoption pending functional data.

### ATLAS Collaboration
- **Bottom line:** Definitive IPD meta-analysis (n=1,886). Adjusted shift OR 1.63 (1.42–1.87). Consistent across subgroups.
- **Practice impact:** Practice-confirming.

### TRIDENT
- **Bottom line (parent):** ~40% RRR for recurrent stroke after spontaneous ICH with low-dose triple-pill, 9 mmHg SBP reduction, no excess ICH. NEJM 2026.
- **Cognitive substudy at ESOC 2026:** Specific numerical results not yet in indexed press.

### EXTEND-IA DNase, LATE-MT, MILD-MT, TNK-MeVO
- **Status:** Confirmed presented; numerical primary-endpoint results not in indexed sources at extraction time. See section 7.

### Pantoja-Ruiz / SLSR
- **Bottom line:** 13% rise in stroke incidence 2020–2024 reverses 30-year decline; ethnic disparities (IRR 2.31 Black African, 2.00 Black Caribbean vs White). Hypothesis-generating; clear policy signal.
- **Confounding:** Single UK region, 95% CIs not in press summary.

### Penn et al. UK Biobank
- **Bottom line:** U-shaped HR-stroke risk (nadir 60–69 bpm). Confounding by indication (rate-controlling drugs, AF, HF) is the major threat to the bradycardia signal.
